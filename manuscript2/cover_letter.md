# Cover Letter — Paper 2 (target: Cancer Immunology Research / JITC / npj Breast Cancer)

Dear Editors,

Please consider our manuscript, **"Triple-negative breast cancer stem cells retain antigen presentation and upregulate the innate-immune checkpoint CD47,"** for publication.

Cancer stem cells (CSCs) drive recurrence and are widely presumed to evade immunity, with the mechanism commonly assumed to be loss of MHC-I antigen presentation, an immune-cold niche, or PD-1/PD-L1 engagement. Testing these assumptions directly across transcriptomic, spatial, and protein modalities in human breast cancer, we find that none holds: CSC-high malignant cells *retain* antigen presentation, are not spatially excluded from T-cells, and do not rely on PD-L1. Instead — specifically in triple-negative breast cancer — CSCs upregulate the innate-immune "don't-eat-me" checkpoint **CD47**, while concordantly losing the CD24 phagocytosis checkpoint (as required by the CD44⁺CD24⁻ definition).

We highlight three features likely to interest your readers:

1. **A reframing** of CSC immune evasion in TNBC from an adaptive/antigen-loss model to an innate/phagocytosis-checkpoint (CD47) model.
2. **Independent replication**: the two core findings (CD47 upregulation and MHC-I retention) replicate in two independent TNBC scRNA-seq cohorts (Pal et al. 2021; Gao et al. 2021), using two different malignant-cell-calling strategies.
3. **A specific therapeutic hypothesis**: because CSCs remain T-cell-visible but upregulate CD47, the data nominate anti-CD47 (magrolimab) — potentially with concurrent T-cell engagement — over anti-PD-1 for CSC-directed immunotherapy in TNBC.

The study is computational and its therapeutic nominations are hypotheses for functional testing, which we state explicitly. All data are public and code is openly available. The manuscript is original, not published elsewhere, and not under consideration by another journal. The author declares no competing interests.
